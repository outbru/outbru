# Bruno Moreira Guedes

Retired as software engineer and as linguistics researcher.

For many years, my professional and academic life revolved around technology, software development, communication and analytical research environments.

My background included exposure to different technical ecosystems and operational realities, from Linux-based infrastructure and open source environments to enterprise-oriented tooling, cross-platform software workflows and business technology environments.

Over time, however, I became increasingly interested in the strategic and organizational side of technology rather than technical specialization itself.

The rise of AI, automation and increasingly accessible technical tooling significantly changed my perspective on long-term professional differentiation. I gradually realized that communication, adaptability, business understanding and strategic thinking were becoming more valuable than isolated technical depth alone.

At the same time, I also grew disillusioned with environments excessively focused on abstraction, internal technical validation and theoretical specialization while remaining disconnected from execution and real-world operational impact.

Because of that, my interests shifted toward:
- business strategy
- digital transformation
- AI adoption
- organizational adaptability
- communication
- market-oriented thinking
- practical execution

I no longer identify primarily as a developer or academic researcher, since I'm much more interested now in market, business, changing things in the real world, etc.

Today I’m much more interested in how businesses, people and technology interact in practical environments than in technical specialization for its own sake.

This profile now exists mostly as an archive of previous projects and interests from another stage of life. I deleted code repositories since I no longer want to maintain them, I really moved forward.
